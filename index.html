Guangzhou Tower, also known as Canton Tower, is a landmark building in Guangzhou. It is the tallest building in China and the third tallest in the world, after the Burj Khalifa in Dubai and the Shanghai Tower. The tower is located in the center of Guangzhou, on the banks of the Pearl River, and can be seen from all over the city. The tower was built in 2009 and opened to the public in 2010. It is 600 meters high, with 112 floors. The tower's design is unique, with a strong modern feel, which symbolizes the development and vitality of Guangzhou. Guangzhou Tower is not only a landmark building, but also a comprehensive tourism attraction. On the observation deck of the tower, you can enjoy the panoramic view of Guangzhou, as well as the beautiful scenery of the Pearl River at night. In addition, there are also restaurants and shopping malls in the tower, providing tourists with more services and experiences. In general, Guangzhou Tower is a very attractive place for tourists to visit, not only because of its unique design and high altitude, but also because it can provide tourists with a variety of experiences and feelings.

The Potala Palace is a historical building located in Lhasa, Tibet. It was originally built as the winter palace of the Tibetan King and is now a well-preserved national key cultural relics under state protection. The Potala Palace has a history of more than a thousand years and is one of the most important symbols of Tibetan culture. It is also one of the most important tourist attractions in Tibet. The building is known for its unique architectural style, exquisite carvings and rich cultural heritage. The Potala Palace has also been recognized as one of the best preserved ancient buildings in China. Visitors can not only appreciate the magnificent scenery of the Potala Palace, but also learn about the history and culture of Tibet.

The Ruins of St. Paul's, commonly known as "Sanba" in Macao, is a historical landmark in Macao. It was originally a Jesuit missionary church built in 1602 to commemorate the first Jesuit missionary to China, Saint Paul. The Ruins of St. Paul's is a fusion of European Renaissance and Oriental architecture, with a unique architectural style and high artistic value. It is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Macao, attracting millions of tourists every year. Visitors can visit the Ruins of St. Paul's and view its historical and cultural significance, as well as the beautiful scenery of Macao.
